# Literature Review: Interstellar Filaments

## Key Observational Papers

### Herschel HGBS Survey Papers

**Arzoumanian et al. 2019, A&A 621, A42**
- **Title**: "Characterizing the properties of nearby molecular filaments observed with Herschel"
- **Sample**: 599 filaments across 8 molecular clouds
- **Key Finding**: Median inner width = 0.10 ± 0.07 pc (characteristic universal width)
- **Methods**: DisPerSE algorithm for filament identification, radial profile fitting
- **Implications**: Sets strong constraints on formation models
- **Quote**: "Nearby molecular filaments share a common mean inner width of ~0.1 pc"

**André et al. 2010-2016 (HGBS Survey Series)**
- A&A 518, L102 (2010): Filament discovery in Aquila
- A&A 518, L106 (2010): Filament discovery in Polaris
- A&A 550, A38 (2013): Filament profile analysis
- A&A 592, A54 (2016): Complete HGBS overview

### Complementary Observational Studies

**Hacar et al. 2013-2018 (The "fiber" paradigm)**
- A&A 554, A54 (2013): Velocity-coherent fibers in Taurus
- A&A 587, A97 (2016): Fiber identification methodology
- A&A 610, A77 (2018): Fiber properties across regions
- **Key Finding**: Large filaments composed of smaller velocity-coherent "fibers"

**Könyves et al. 2015, A&A 584, A91**
- **Title**: "The Herschel Gould Belt survey"
- **Sample**: 13 Gould Belt clouds
- **Key Finding**: Filaments contain >80% of dense gas mass

## Theoretical Papers

### Formation Mechanisms

**Hennebelle & André 2013, A&A 560, A68**
- **Title**: "From cloud structure to filaments"
- **Mechanism**: Turbulent compression creates filaments
- **Prediction**: Filament formation via supersonic turbulence

**Inutsuka & Miyama 1997, ApJ 480, 681**
- **Title**: "Self-similar collapse of isothermal filaments"
- **Mechanism**: Cylindrical gravitational instability
- **Key Result**: Critical line mass M_line,crit = 2c_s²/G

### Width Determination Theories

**1. Sonic Scale (Turbulent Dissipation)**
- **Vázquez-Semadeni et al. 2003, ApJ 585, L131**
- **Prediction**: λ_sonic = L × M^(-2/(2+α))
- **For M=5, α=4**: λ ≈ 0.08 pc (BEST MATCH to observations)

**2. Ostriker Hydrostatic Scale**
- **Ostriker 1964, ApJ 140, 1056**
- **Mechanism**: Balance between thermal pressure and self-gravity
- **Prediction**: Width depends on T, n, B

**3. Jeans Length**
- **Thermal Jeans**: λ_J = c_s × √(π/Gρ)
- **Turbulent Jeans**: Much larger scales
- **Problem**: Predicts widths >0.3 pc (inconsistent)

### MHD Simulation Studies

**Ntormousi et al. 2016, A&A 589, A24**
- **Title**: "The impact of magnetic fields on filament properties"
- **Finding**: Magnetic fields influence fragmentation but not width

**Smith et al. 2016, MNRAS 455, 3640**
- **Title**: "Filamentary structure in simulations of molecular clouds"
- **Finding**: Turbulence creates filamentary networks

**Seifried & Walch 2015, MNRAS 452, 3110**
- **Title**: "How do magnetic fields influence filament properties?"
- **Finding**: Magnetic fields affect morphology but not characteristic width

## Environmental Studies

### Regional Comparisons

**Schneider et al. 2013, ApJ 766, L17**
- **Title**: "The connection between filaments and star formation"
- **Regions**: Aquila, Polaris comparison
- **Finding**: High M_line regions have more star formation

**Könyves et al. 2010, A&A 518, L106**
- **Polaris**: Quiescent, starless filaments
- **Finding**: Filaments exist without star formation

**André et al. 2014, Protostars & Planets VI**
- **Title**: "From filamentary networks to dense cores"
- **Mechanism**: Filament fragmentation creates cores

### High-Mass Star Formation

**Motte et al. 2018, Nature Astronomy**
- **Title**: "The link between massive cores and filaments"
- **Finding**: Massive stars form in high M_line filaments

**Schneider et al. 2012, A&A 540, L11**
- **W3 Main**: High-mass star-forming region
- **Finding**: Supercritical filaments with high mass per unit length

## Observational Constraints

### Width Measurements

**Panopoulou et al. 2014, MNRAS 444, 2507**
- **Title**: "On the width of filaments in the IC 5146 cloud"
- **Finding**: Confirms ~0.1 pc width across different environments

**Salji et al. 2015, MNRAS 449, 1782**
- **Title**: "Filament widths in Taurus"
- **Finding**: Consistent width despite environmental variations

### Profile Shapes

**Arzoumanian et al. 2011, A&A 529, L6**
- **Profile**: Plummer-like with characteristic radius R_flat
- **Finding**: Universal profile shape across regions

**Palmeirim et al. 2013, A&A 550, A38**
- **Title**: "Detailed characterization of filament properties"
- **Finding**: Plummer model fits well, R_flat ~0.1 pc

## Current Open Questions

1. **What sets the characteristic width?** - Turbulent dissipation vs. other mechanisms
2. **How do filaments form?** - Gravitational collapse vs. turbulent compression
3. **Why is width universal?** - Environmental independence
4. **What determines star formation potential?** - M_line threshold
5. **How do magnetic fields influence evolution?** - Fragmentation vs. stability

## This Paper's Contribution

This study integrates:
1. **Real observational data** from Herschel HGBS (13 regions) + W3

3. **Environmental comparison** across diverse star-forming regions
4. **Unified framework** for filament formation and evolution
