## P0 Fixes — Factual Errors (MUST FIX)

### Fix 1: Pantheon+ sample size (3 locations)
- **Problem:** Paper stated "1,701 Type Ia supernovae" — incorrect; Pantheon+ has 1,701 *light curves* but only **1,550 distinct** Type Ia SNe.
- **Sections fixed:** §5.2 body text (L1132), Fig. 10 caption (L475), Conclusion §10 (L1514)
- **Change:** "1,701 Type Ia supernovae" → "1,701 light curves of 1,550 distinct Type Ia supernovae" (body); "1,550 distinct Type Ia supernovae" elsewhere
- **Source:** Brout et al. (2022) explicitly state 1,550 distinct SNe Ia

### Fix 2: Downsizing misattribution (§4.3, L732)
- **Problem 1:** `\citep{Brinchmann2004}` was cited for "downsizing" — wrong paper; Brinchmann (2004) is the MPA-JHU catalogue paper, not the downsizing paper.
- **Problem 2:** Characterization "form stars less efficiently" is scientifically incorrect. Downsizing means massive galaxies formed stars *earlier* (higher redshift).
- **Change:** Citation → `\citep{Cowie1996}` (Cowie et al. 1996 ApJ 112, 839, who coined the term). Characterization → "formed their stars earlier, at higher redshift"
- **Note:** Brinchmann (2004) correctly remains as the MPA-JHU catalogue reference elsewhere.
- **New bib entry added:** `Cowie1996`

### Fix 3: Sonic scale misattribution (§3.4 interpretation, L626)
- **Problem:** `\citep{Andre2010}` cited for the sonic scale interpretation of ~0.1 pc filament widths. André et al. (2010) do NOT discuss the sonic scale; the word "sonic" does not appear in that paper.
- **Fix:** Citation → `\citep{Arzoumanian2011}`, who introduced this interpretation: "This characteristic width of ~0.1 pc corresponds to within a factor of ~2 to the sonic scale below which interstellar turbulence becomes subsonic."
- **Arzoumanian2011 already in bibliography** (no new entry needed)

## P1 Fixes — Misleading Claims (SHOULD FIX)

### Fix 4: Arzoumanian (2019) σ comparison (§3.3, L602)
- **Problem:** "consistent with the range reported in recent Herschel analyses (Arzoumanian et al. 2019)" was misleading — Arzoumanian (2019) report IQR = 0.07 pc, not σ in dex; no direct comparison is possible.
- **Change:** Explicitly labels σ = 0.19 dex as "ASTRA's fitted value"; states Arzoumanian (2019) report "a median width of 0.10 pc with an interquartile range of 0.07 pc from a larger Herschel sample, though the statistics are not directly comparable."

### Fix 5: Panopoulou (2022) understatement (§3.3, L603 and Fig. 1 caption)
- **Problem:** Paper said Panopoulou (2022) "discuss resolution effects" — understating a definitive conclusion. They *explicitly demonstrate* that 0.1 pc universality is a resolution artifact.
- **Change (body):** Rewritten to: "the universality of this characteristic width has been challenged by Panopoulou et al. (2022), who demonstrate that measured filament widths scale with distance and effective beam size, suggesting the 0.1 pc value may partly reflect instrumental resolution rather than an intrinsic physical scale."
- **Change (Fig. 1 caption):** Updated to "who argue this value may partly reflect instrumental resolution"

### Fix 6: André (2010) 4× width → IMF peak argument (§3.4, L627)
- **Problem:** `\citep{Andre2010}` was cited for the specific "4× width → 0.4 pc → IMF peak" quantitative chain. André (2010) discusses CMF-IMF resemblance but NOT this specific argument.
- **Change:** Citation updated to `\citep{Andre2014}` (André et al. 2014, Protostars & Planets VI, which does discuss the filament-IMF connection more explicitly). Framing changed to "a connection explored in the observational literature (e.g. André et al. 2014)" — presenting it as ASTRA's analysis supported by the literature.
- **New bib entry added:** `Andre2014`

### Fix 7: Strateva colour index mismatch (§5.4, L1145)
- **Problem:** Text said ASTRA's u−g bimodality was "consistent with the colour separation reported by Strateva et al. (2001)." Strateva (2001) used u*−r* (separator at 2.22), which is not directly comparable to u−g.
- **Change:** Added explicit note: "We note that Strateva et al. (2001) identified galaxy colour bimodality using u*−r* (separator at u*−r* = 2.22), which is not directly comparable to our u−g analysis." Primary bimodality citation changed to Baldry et al. (2004) who studied full colour-magnitude bimodality across multiple colours.
- **New bib entry added:** `Baldry2004`
