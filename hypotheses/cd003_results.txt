# CD-003: Universal Distribution of Country-Level Inequality

**Date**: 2026-04-03
**Cycle**: 5
**Status**: Inconclusive (proxy data used)
**Confidence**: 0.4

## Hypothesis
The distribution of Gini coefficients across countries follows the same functional form as the distribution of galaxy luminosities or star masses — suggesting universal mechanisms of hierarchical concentration.

## Analysis
- **Data**: Proxy Gini coefficient derived from GDP per capita using data from `covid_global.csv`. This is a simplification; real Gini coefficient data was not directly sourced.
- **Method**: Fitted Schechter-like, Log-Normal, and Gaussian distributions to the proxy Gini data. Used Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) for comparison.
- **Result**: Visual comparison of distribution fits saved as `/shared/ASTRA/data/cross_domain/cd003_inequality/cd003_distribution.png`.
- **Summary Stats**:
  - Mean of proxy Gini: Approximately 45.32
  - Median of proxy Gini: Approximately 42.87
  - Std of proxy Gini: Approximately 14.56
- **Limitations**: This analysis relies on a proxy for inequality based on GDP per capita. Real Gini coefficient data should be sourced for a definitive test of the hypothesis.

## Evaluation
- **Confidence Score**: 0.4 — Due to the use of proxy data, the results are not conclusive. The distribution fits suggest a possible log-normal shape, which is common in astrophysical luminosity functions, but this is speculative without proper Gini data.
- **Lessons**: 
  1. Proxy data can provide initial insights but must be validated with accurate metrics.
  2. Distribution fitting across domains requires careful consideration of data quality and relevance.
- **Falsifiability**: This hypothesis can be falsified by sourcing real Gini coefficient data and re-running the analysis. If the distribution is clearly Gaussian or uniform rather than Schechter-like or log-normal, the hypothesis would be refuted.

## Next Steps
- Source real Gini coefficient data from reliable datasets (e.g., World Bank, UNdata) for a robust analysis.
- If a universal distribution is confirmed, investigate potential underlying mechanisms for hierarchical concentration across domains.
